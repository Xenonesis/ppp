BOOT (Build-Own-Operate-Transfer)

Definition & Key Features

A PPP model where a private entity builds, owns, and operates an infrastructure facility for a specified period before transferring ownership to the public sector

Extended timeframe (25-30 years) Temporary ownership Revenue during operation
Private financing of construction costs
Temporary ownership by private entity
Mandatory transfer to public sector at end

BOOT Lifecycle

BUILD
Private sector finances and constructs the facility
OWN
Private entity owns temporarily during concession
OPERATE
Private entity operates and collects revenue
TRANSFER
Ownership transferred to public sector
