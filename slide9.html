Concession

Definition & Key Features

A contractual arrangement where the public authority grants rights to a private entity to operate, maintain, and sometimes develop a public asset or service

Long-term (15-30 years) Public ownership maintained Revenue collection rights
Significant risk transfer to private sector
Commercial autonomy for concessionaire
Performance obligations with penalties

Real-World Examples

Toll Roads

France's Autoroutes system with 9,000km of highways operated under concession agreements, generating €8B annually

Port Facilities

Port of Rotterdam concession to Hutchison Ports increased container throughput by 35% over 10 years

Airports

Heathrow Airport's 30-year concession resulted in £11B investment and 20% increase in passenger capacity

Water Utilities

Buenos Aires water concession achieved 95% service coverage and 70% reduction in waterborne diseases
